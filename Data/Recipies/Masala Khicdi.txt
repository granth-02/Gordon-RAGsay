Name: Masala Khicdi 
Cuisine: Indian
Type: Dinner
Prep Time: 20mins
Cook TIme: 25mins
Spice Level: 5/5
Savoury Level: 5/5
Salt Level: 4/5
Sweetness Level: 1/5
Umami Level: 3/5 
Sweetness type: Natural from veggies
Main Flavour profile: Packed with indian spices and garlicky taste
Serves: 3

Ingredients:
Toor Dal/split pigeon pea ½ cup
Masoor Dal/red lentils ½ cup
Rice 1 Cup
Onion 1 to 1.5 piece 
Tomato 1 to 1.5 piece
Capsicum ½ piece
Carrot ½ piece
Peas ¼ cup
Green beans ¼ cup
Potatoes 1
Corn ¼ cup
Green Chillie 1-2 piece
Garlic cloves 6 pieces
Half Lemon juice
Indian Spices (Red Chilli powder 2 teaspoon, turmeric 1 teaspoon, Cummin seeds ½  teaspoon, Coriander Powder 2 teaspoons, Garam Masala ¼ teaspoon, Sunday Masala 1 teaspoon, Khichdi Masala 1 tsp)
Salt 2 to 2.5 teaspoon
Oil 2-2.5 tablespoon

Procedure:
Onions should be finely diced cut
Carots, green beans, potatoes can be diced cut or kept long
Soak both daal in water for 15-20 mins
Add dal and rice together into a pressure cooker, add 2 tps of salt, ½ teaspoon of turmeric powder and corn
Cook them on high heat for 4 whistles
Tadka Recipie
Onions, Garlic and Tomatoes should be diced and finely cut
Add Oil to the pan, let it heat
Add Chopped garlic cloves, green chilli and Cummin seeds, Dried chilli
Let the Gloves get slightly brown to brown
Add Onions, saute until they are slightly brown to brown
Add in the remaining spices and ½  tsp of salt
Saute and mix well, then add rest of the vegies
Cookwell until mixed well
Then add the tadka to the cooked dal and rice in pressure cooker, mix well
Add water according to preference 
Serve in a bowl, garnish with coriander and sprinkle lime juice on top

My Notes: I like to add more garlic to cook it a little longer than required to enure its taste is prevalent in the dish as a lot of different spices are being added. The green chilli helps in giving the dish natural spice. I love adding more vegetables in every dish of mine, the make the dish very fresh and fibrous. The sweetness or hint of sweetness comes from carrots, peas and potatoes which is more than enough for this dish, the carmelised onions also add to the sweetness. Also I like my khichdi being soft and mushy.

Alternatives:
In vegetables anything can be used but onion tomatoes and potatoes are a must
Green Moong Dal instead of toor dal

Additional Options:
Spinach 
Cauliflower 
For more protein add in boiled soya chunks about ¼ cup
