Name: One Pot Chole/chickpea Curry
Cuisine: Indian
Type: Lunch/Dinner
Prep Time: 8 hours (soaking chole/chickpeas) + 20 mins
Cook TIme: 25mins
Spice Level: 5/5
Savoury Level: 5/5
Salt Level: 4/5
Sweetness Level: 1/5 
Umami Level: 3/5 
Sweetness type: Natural from onions
Main Flavour profile: Packed with indian spices and garlicky, earthy taste
Serves: 3

Ingredients:
Chole/Chickpeas 1 Cup
Onions 2-3 pieces
Tomatoes 1-2 pieces
Garlic Cloves 6-7
Indian Spices (Red Chilli powder 2 teaspoon, turmeric ½  teaspoon, Cummin seeds ½  teaspoon, Black Peppercons ½ teaspoon, Coriander Powder 2 teaspoons, Garam Masala ¼ teaspoon, Sunday Masala 1.5 teaspoon, Chole Masala 1.5 tsp, Kitchen King Masala 1 tsp)
Dried Red Chilli 4-5 pieces
Bay leaves 2-3 pieces
Tea 1 tsp
Salt 2-3 tsp
Kastoori Methi ½ tablespoon
Oil 3-5 tbsp
Lemon ½ 

Procedure:
Soak Chole/chickpeas in water overnight or 8 hours
In pressure cooker ad 3-5 tbsp of oil and let it heat
Then add peppercorns, chopped garlic cloves, dried red chillies, bay leaves, cummin seeds and saute until garlic is brown in color
Add chopped onions (juillien of half moons), now let them saute till they are dark brown
Add in the spices and mix well, the base should look deep dark brown
Then add diced tomatoes and mix will
Add the soaked chickpeas and mix well with the curry base
Add water a little over the point till the chole are submerged
Optional Step: Before adding water boil it with tea and then add the water to the pressure cooker
Let it cook for 5-6 whistles
Add in the crushed kastoori methi and sqeed half a lemon
Serve it


My Notes: The main reason behind adding tea water to cook chole is to give it a high umami taste and black color, but I can achieve it without it. What I essentially do is really caremalize my onions and garlic till they are dark brown and then mix it with the spices. Due to this step the base of the curry already looks brown and the burnt onions give it a great umami and sweet taste to the curry, whilst the garlic balances all the spices. Due to this step one can skip the tea part, although I still add the tea as it elevates the entire dish to a whole different level of spice, umami and savoury flavours.

Alternatives:
No dried red chilli → extra red chilli powder, but add it later so it doesn't burn
No kasuri methi → skip it, nothing replicates it exactly but dried fenugreek seeds (half the quantity) get close
Want it more tangy → extra half lemon or a spoon of tamarind paste with the tomatoes 
Want it creamier → spoon of yoghurt stirred in after the last whistle
