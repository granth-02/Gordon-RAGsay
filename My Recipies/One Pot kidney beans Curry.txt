Name: One Pot Rajma/kidney beans Curry
Cuisine: Indian
Type: Lunch/Dinner
Prep Time: 8 hours (soaking Rajma/kidney) + 20 mins
Cook TIme: 25mins
Spice Level: 3/5
Savoury Level: 4/5
Salt Level: 3/5
Sweetness Level: 2/5 
Umami Level: 3/5 
Sweetness type: Natural from onions
Main Flavour profile: Packed with indian spices, earthy taste with backend sweetness
Serves: 3

Ingredients:
Rajma/kidney 1 Cup
Onions 2-3 pieces
Tomatoes 2-3 pieces (same as onions)
Garlic Cloves 6-7
Indian Spices (Red Chilli powder 2 teaspoon, turmeric ½  teaspoon, Cummin seeds ½  teaspoon, Black Peppercons ½ teaspoon, Coriander Powder 2 teaspoons, Garam Masala ¼ teaspoon, Sunday Masala 1.5 teaspoon, Kitchen King Masala 1 tsp)
Dried Red Chilli 4-5 pieces
Bay leaves 2-3 pieces
Salt 2-3 tsp
Kastoori Methi ½ tablespoon
Oil 3-5 tbsp
Lemon ½ 

Procedure:
Soak Chole/chickpeas in water overnight or 8 hours
In pressure cooker ad 3-5 tbsp of oil and let it heat
Then add peppercorns, chopped garlic cloves, dried red chillies, bay leaves, cummin seeds and saute until garlic is brown in color
Add chopped onions (juillien of half moons), now let them saute till they are dark brown
Add in the spices and mix well, the base should look deep dark brown
Then add diced tomatoes and mix will
Add the soaked kidney beans and mix well with the curry base
Add water a little over the point till the chole are submerged
Let it cook for 5-6 whistles
Add in the crushed kastoori methi and sqeed half a lemon
Serve it


My Notes: Overall I prefer chickpea over rajma but it is a great taste changer in itself, rajma naturally are sweeter and that is shown forth in the dish itself. Here the dominant flavour is not the spices added nor the onion taste rather the kidney beans are the hero. Overall its spicy, sweet, tangy and earthy. 

Alternatives:
No dried red chilli → extra red chilli powder, but add it later so it doesn't burn
No kasuri methi → skip it
Want it more tangy → extra half lemon or a spoon of tamarind paste with the tomatoes
