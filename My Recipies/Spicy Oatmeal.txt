Name: Spicy Oatmeal
Cusine: Indian
Type: Breakfast
Prep Time: 10mins
Cook TIme: 15mins
Spice Level: 5/5
Savoury Level: 5/5
Salt Level: 4/5
Sweetness Level: 1/5
Sweetness type: Natural from veggies
Main Flavour profile: Packed with indian spices and garlicky taste
Serves: 2

Ingrediants:
Rolled oats 90g
Onion 1 piece 
Tomato 1 piece
Capsicum ½ piece
Carrot ½ piece
Peas ¼ cup
Potatoes 1
Green Chillie 1-2 piece
Garlic cloves 4 pieces
Half Lemon juice
Indian Spices (Red Chilli powder 1.5 teaspoon, turmeric ½ teaspoon, Poppy seeds ¼ teaspoon)
Peri Peri powder ½ tablespoon
Salt 1 to 1.5 teaspoon
Oil 1-1.5 tablespoon

Procedure:
All the vegies should be diced cut
Add Oil to the pan, let it heat
Add Chopped garlic cloves, green chilli and poppy seeds
Let the Gloves get golden to slightly brown
Add Onions, saute until they are golden to slightly brown 
Add in the remaining spices (chilli powder, turmeric, Peri Peri powder)
Saute and mix well, then add rest of the vegetables
Cookwell until mixed well
Add in the oats and mix well
Add ½ glass of water and soften the oats
Add more water to make it more soupy
Serve in a bowl, garnish with coriander and sprinkle lime juice on top

My Notes: I like to add more garlic to cook it a little longer than required to enure its taste is prevalent in the dish as a lot of different spices are being added. The green chilli helps in giving the dish natural spice. I love adding more vegetables in every dish of mine, the make the dish very fresh and fibrous. The sweetness or hint of sweetness comes from carrots, peas and potatoes which is more than enough for this dish. Also I like my oats being soft and mushy yet solid not watery of soupy so I add less water

Alternatives:
In vegetables anything can be used but onion tomatoes and potatoes are a must

Additional Options:
Spinach 
Chilli flakes for more spice
Brocoli
Green Beans
