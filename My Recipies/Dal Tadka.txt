Name: Dal Tadka
Cusine: Indian
Type: Lunch/Dinner
Prep Time: 30mins
Cook TIme: 15mins
Spice Level: 5/5
Savoury Level: 5/5
Salt Level: 4/5
Sweetness Level: 1/5
Sweetness type: Natural from veggies
Main Flavour profile: Packed with indian spices and garlicky taste
Serves: 3

Ingrediants:
Toor Dal/split pigeon pea ½ cup
Masoor Dal/red lentils ½ cup
Onion 1 piece 
Tomato 1 piece
Green Chilli 1-2 piece
Garlic cloves 6 pieces
Half Lemon juice
Indian Spices (Red Chilli powder 2 teaspoon, turmeric 1 teaspoon, Cummin seeds ½  teaspoon, Coriander Powder 2 teaspoons, Garam Masala ¼ teaspoon, Sunday Masala 1 teaspoon)
Dried Red chillies 4-5 piece 
Salt 2 to 2.5 teaspoon
Oil 2-2.5 tablespoon

Procedure:
Soak both daal in water for 15-20 mins
Add them into a pressure cooker, add 2 tps of salt, ½ teaspoon of turmeric powder
Cook them on high heat for 4 whistles
Tadka Recipie
Onions, Garlic and Tomatoes should be diced and finely cut
Add Oil to the pan, let it heat
Add Chopped garlic cloves, green chilli and Cummin seeds, Dried chilli
Let the Gloves get slightly brown to brown
Add Onions, saute until they are slightly brown to brown
Add in the remaining spices and ½  tsp of salt
Saute and mix well, then add tomatoes
Cookwell until mixed well
Then add the tadka to the cooked dal in pressure cooker, mix well
Add water according to preference 
Serve in a bowl, garnish with coriander and sprinkle lime juice on top

My Notes: The amount of garlic is high and is cooked for a longer period as the burnt garlic brings out the smokey flavour of the dal that perfectly pairs with the high amount of spice. The sweetness comes from the onions as I let the carmelise a little longer till they are brown as it brings out the natruat oniony sweetness without the need of adding sugar. I like my dal on the thicker side but not too thick its should be soupy yet slurpy, essentially should be able to bite down on few of the lentils.
Alternatives:
Channa Dal 
Urid Dal
Moong Dal 

Additional Options:
Adding Spinach in the pressure cooker with dal will make a new dish called dal palak
